SimpliSell

Buying and selling -- simplified!

SimpliSell is a revolutionary new buying and selling platform designed for student use. Featuring a tailored suite of categories directly related to student needs, such as transportation, living arrangements, and more.

We take the hassle out of trying to find the right buyer for your old laptop, or finding a used copy of that one specific textbook required for your algorithms course by advertising directly where students are the most -- on campus.

A SimpliSell terminal is more than just a bulletin board for advertisements, it's a way to bring the student community closer than ever before, with clubs and events able to directly reach more students than any other alternative!


Current Features:


User Login

A robust and secure login page with custom security questions just in case you forget your password.

Advertisement Posting

Only one click and you can be creating an advertisement in seconds, designed for ease of use.

Searching

The ability to sort by price and category type has never been easier!


Persistent Storage

Your advertisements are saved in our highly efficient and reliable HSQLDB back end.

User Profiles

Customize your own profile and contact details so other users know how to contact you!

Reporting

See an offensive ad? Report it!


Admin Controls

Investigate what advertisements have been reported with the option to delete them if necessary.

Advertisement Expiry

Don't let old ads hog up space! Forgotten ads will expire after 3 weeks unless they have been reposted.

Reposting

Is your ad still relevant? Repost it with just one click!

Our Vision:

SimpliSell will be a classified advertisement platform designed to give students a centralized place to post and view local advertisements.

The application will be akin to a digital bulletin board comprising of advertisements for a variety of different things. Users will be able to post advertisements to sell textbooks, devices like laptops, tickets to community/school events, tutoring services, or even information regarding student clubs or groups. They will also have the option to create "wanted" advertisements, such as in the case of looking for other students to carpool with. Users will be able to simply navigate through this list of existing ad postings, and will be able to easily obtain the advertiser's contact information for further communication.

Users will be able to search for advertisements based on categories, while also having the ability to sort postings based on price. Users can save advertisements for later by adding specific postings to their watch list. A rating system will be put in place that will ask users to provide feedback on their interactions with other users. Additionally, users will be able to report inappropriate advertisements or users, which will be further investigated by an administrator.

SimpliSell is designed primarily for students, so users will expect advertisements that are catered to their own desires and needs. Therefore, instead of having an overwhelming and abundant amount of unrelated advertisements, SimpliSell gives students a user-friendly platform that allows them to navigate through advertisements they are more likely to be interested in.

To increase convenience for students, the application will be stationed within educational institutions to maximise exposure towards our targeted audience, which are students. To reflect this very demographic, advertisement postings will be classified according to various categories primarily tailored for items or services used in educational institutions. Students will also be able to post advertisements for anything related to their institution, such as volunteer groups or gatherings.

The suitable location will prove advantageous to advertisers as well since the constant exposure to students may result in an increase in response rate and increase in sales. To further aid their effort, the always on display feature will scroll through all the existing ad postings so that students walking by the application can see them. With the rating system and reporting system built in the application, students can also feel at ease when doing business with trusted individuals on the platform.

The application will be considered successful based on the following criteria. First, the majority of students prefer using SimpliSell over existing advertisement posting services, as determined by a survey. Second, users posting advertisements on the system see at least a 25% increase in genuinely interested responses compared to alternative platforms. Lastly, there is a demand for additional SimpliSell terminals within the current or other educational institutions.

Development Postmortem:

The development of SimpliSell was a rather smooth experience. Throughout the development process our team communicated regularly to one another via online chat programs and we had an in-person meeting at least once a week. Each member of the team took on an equal amount of work, and all were eager to get work done. When a team member was having difficulty with an assigned task they undertook, others were always available and willing to lend a hand. We did not have any major disagreements with the development process or priorities for the application, and any constructive criticism a team member received was taken seriously by that team member and rectified immediately. We attribute our communication and teamwork as the largest contributor to the success of our application.

However the development process was not without any hiccups.

The first problem we encountered was the issue with estimating work. No one in our development group had worked with the development tools in this project before. Therefore trying to estimate how much work that needs to be done, on a project you have no experience in, is difficult. Some tasks were highly over or under budgeted on time, leading some developers to have a misunderstanding on how much work had to be done. Thankfully our team was flexible and we regularly would re-distribute tasks mid development. Oddly enough, the over and under budgeting regularly evened out in the end, leading to an overall development time that was on track with overall estimates.

Another issue was our commit schedule. We would regularly do our work locally and only commit to the origin branches on task completion. This was not an issue at most times, as some tasks were completely unrelated to one another. However, sometimes tasks needed parts of another task to be completed before they could be implemented. This was brought up in our iteration 2 retrospective, and will be discussed in more detail below.

Lastly, while we communicated regularly about the tasks we would be doing. While development was underway more often than not we were silent about our work progress. This lead to some confusion in the middle of our iterations about what work has been completed and what is still left. This issue directly ties into the previous one. With more frequent commits, we would be indirectly communicating with the rest of the group on development progress more regularly.

One of our iteration 2 retrospective areas of improvement was with the way we handle task distribution and updates to work being done. Sometimes, in order to start development on a task, the previous one relating to it would need to have been implemented or at least partially implemented. This lead to issues where developers could not start on their assigned work until the previous developer committed their finished implementation. We amended this problem in iteration 3 by carefully assigning tasks which should not overlap with other developers tasks if possible, breaking up tasks into smaller more manageable pieces, and also remind ourselves to commit early, and often. This change lead to a fluid development process for the final iteration of our project.

We did have one bug tracked (marked as resolved now due to it being a known issue), which we are unsure how to even fix in its current implementation. Upon introducing the HSQLDB persistence layer, we started getting massive performance issues with our application. It is known that HSQLDB is rather slow on an android device, however on the physical device, it could at times take 10 seconds to log into the application. Some refactoring of redundant database calls did make a dent in this time, however it is still on the slow side. We believe perhaps implementing a different persistence back end would fix this. SQLite is native on android. If we could start this project over, we believe that moving to a native database implementation could drastically improve performance. We have discussed this with other teams currently using SQLite, and they state that performance has been a lot faster than what we are currently experiencing with HSQLDB.

The original project vision has not changed since iteration 0. The only things that have changed would be re-prioritizing features and delaying the ones with the lowest priority due to time constraints. At the end of the project we completed 9 out of our originally planned 12 total features:

  • User Login
  • Advertisement Posting
  • Searching
  • Persistent Storage
  • User Profiles
  • Reporting
  • Admin Controls
  • Ad Expiry
  • Reposting

The 3 features that did not get implemented are as follows:

  • Automatic Advertisement Scrolling
  • Watchlist
  • Rating System

As a team we have learned that large projects such as this require a great deal of clear communication. If we forgot to document something in detail while communicating in person, others can misinterpret what was agreed upon and lead to implementing something entirely different than expected. We found that we would hash out most ideas in person, then later on in the iteration there would be some confusion as to how exactly something is to be implemented. Whereas if we documented clearly during these discussions on what is to be done, there would be no confusion. This works best if you know how something should be implemented beforehand, but most things we were working with we had no idea how they would fit together until after we have gotten our hands dirty.

In conclusion, we as a team feel the most important aspect of working on a large project is communication. You can have an excellent idea for a project and have everything mapped out perfectly, from tasks to accurate estimates. However, poor communication can make a good project crumble to its knees. Most if not all of the issues we faced in the development of our project are solely due to communication errors. When we brought these issues to light during our retrospectives, development progress seemed to smooth out almost immediately. While we are disappointed that some features had to get cut that near the end of development, we are pleased with how the final iteration of the project came together. Everyone in the group pulled their weight, and we had a lot of fun along the way.

- The SimpliSell Team
